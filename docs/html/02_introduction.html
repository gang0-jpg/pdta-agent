02 Introduction

2.1 Background

Artificial Intelligence has significantly transformed quantitative investment over the past several decades.

Modern investment systems employ statistical models, machine learning algorithms, and large-scale optimization techniques to estimate expected returns, evaluate investment risk, and construct efficient portfolios.

Recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) have further expanded the capabilities of AI-assisted investment by enabling natural-language explanations, market summarization, and interactive decision support.

Despite these advances, most existing investment systems continue to focus primarily on markets and portfolios.

The investor is often treated as a fixed set of parameters—such as risk tolerance or investment horizon—rather than as a dynamic decision maker whose experience, behavior, and objectives evolve over time.

2.2 The Missing Component

Investment decisions are not determined solely by mathematical optimization.

They are influenced by many factors, including:

  • Investment philosophy
  • Personal experience
  • Market perception
  • Psychological bias
  • Risk tolerance
  • Long-term objectives

Two investors presented with the same market data may legitimately reach different investment decisions.

Conventional portfolio optimization frameworks cannot explain these differences because they optimize portfolios rather than model investors.

This observation motivated the development of PDTA.

2.3 From Portfolio Optimization to Investor Modeling

Traditional investment research attempts to answer the following question:

“What is the optimal portfolio?”

PDTA approaches the problem differently.

Instead, it asks:

“Why does this investor choose this portfolio?”

This change of perspective transforms the investment problem from one of numerical optimization into one of behavioral modeling.

Portfolio optimization remains an essential component of PDTA, but it becomes only one part of a broader decision-making framework.

2.4 The PDTA Approach

Portfolio Digital Twin Agent (PDTA) combines quantitative finance with AI Agent technologies to model the investment process itself.

The framework currently integrates:

  • Market data acquisition
  • Portfolio optimization
  • Scenario evaluation
  • Investment recommendation
  • Policy-based adjustment
  • Persistent Investor Memory

Future versions will extend the framework with:

  • Reflection Agents
  • Explainable AI
  • Persona Learning
  • Continuous behavioral adaptation

Together, these components establish the foundation for building an Investor Digital Twin.

2.5 Research Objective

The objective of PDTA is not to replace investors.

Instead, the project seeks to assist investors by preserving historical investment decisions, identifying behavioral patterns, and supporting continuous improvement through AI.

Rather than acting as an autonomous trading system, PDTA is designed as an AI framework that collaborates with human investors.

Its long-term objective is to model how investment decisions are made, how they evolve over time, and how they can be explained.

2.6 Scope of This White Paper

This White Paper presents the design philosophy, architecture, implementation, and future direction of PDTA.

The remainder of this document is organized as follows:

  • Chapter 3 explains the concept of the Investor Digital Twin.
  • Chapter 4 describes the evolution from Silicon Digital Twin to Investor Digital Twin.
